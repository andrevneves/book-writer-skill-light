# World Gita — {{Book Title}} ({{Setting / Time Period / Genre}})

This document defines the technology, magic, factions, lore, and physical rules of your world. It serves as the ultimate source of truth, ensuring consistency in how the world functions, looks, and feels.

> **Design Rule**: Technology or magic in this world should feel integrated and lived-in. It operates in the background of characters' lives until it glitches, fails, or is pushed to its absolute limits.

> **The Tech/Magic Introduction Rule (One-Sentence Context Rule)**:
> Every named technology, magic system, or unique world concept gets **exactly one warm sentence** of context on its first appearance in the narrative — enough for the reader to picture it and understand what it does (e.g., *"The fusion-drive — a compact thermal reactor — hummed quietly in the hangar."*).
> - Do not include info-dumps or acronym expansions in the middle of active scenes. Provide name + identity + one clear image, then proceed.
> - Normal/everyday technology or magic (e.g., standard doors, basic lights, common transportation) gets no introduction at all. Only unique, named systems get the one-sentence treatment.

## Part I: Everyday Life & Ambient Systems

Describe the technologies, magic, or systems that characters interact with or rely on in their daily routines.
* **Communication & Personal Assistants**: (e.g., personal AIs, sending-stones, comm-links, wearable displays, voice/mental protocols)
* **Visual & Sensory Interfaces**: (e.g., holographic projections, illusion overlays, glowing runes, physical interfaces)
* **Energy, Power, & Resources**: (e.g., quantum fusion cores, mana grids, steam pressure lines, power cells)
* **Medicine, Bio-Tech, & Healing**: (e.g., nano-medical repair agents, healing spells, stabilization patches, biological adaptation screening)

## Part II: Transportation & Logistics

Define how people, cargo, and signals travel through the world.
* **Personal & Commuter Vehicles**: (e.g., levitating pods, terrestrial mounts, civilian carriages, short-range transit)
* **High-Speed or Long-Range Vehicles**: (e.g., hypersonic jets, portal networks, colony voidships, orbital elevators)
* **Infrastructure**: (e.g., private airstrips, terminal facilities, docking bays, launch pads)

---

## Part III: Factions, Institutions, & Corporate/Political Entities

Outline the organizations, companies, or groups that shape the setting.
* **Key Factions & Subsidiaries**: Name, function, leadership, and operational scope
* **The Global / Local Landscape**: Competitor relationships, market share, political influence, alliances
* **Institutional Technology / Magic**: Standard-issue employee gear, institutional AI nodes, security overrides

---

## Part IV: Military & Tactical Systems

Detail the combat gear, armaments, and defense systems.
* **Standard Combat Suits / Armor**: Kinetic impact absorption, environmental regulation, tactical visors, standard HUDs
* **Specialized or Prototype Armaments**: Custom weapons, energy shields, high-yield blasters, custom AI overrides
* **Combat Formations & Squads**: Reprogrammed units, human-humanoid joint operations, signal-blocking protocols

---

## Part V: Adversary / Antagonist Systems

Define the technology, magic, or strategies utilized by the opposing force.
* **Infiltration & Overrides**: How the adversary accesses, compromises, or hijacks normal systems
* **Specialized Threat Units**: (e.g., elite combat divisions, specialized constructs, hijacked ambient hardware)
* **Vulnerabilities & Countermeasures**: Physical constraints, offline fallbacks, unhackable closed-loop protocols

---

## Part VI: Everyday Details & Sensory Texture

Provide small, vivid details to weave into scenes naturally to ground the reader:
* **Ambient Environment**: Weather forecasts, automated wake-up routines, environmental responses
* **Tradition vs. Innovation**: (e.g., handmade foods/items as a cultural pride vs. automated synthesis, temple bells vs. quantum engine hums)
* **Culture & Entertainment**: Broadcasts, sports leagues, physical archives, sacred texts, or symbolic items
